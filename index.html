Mission & Goals

Our Mission

To strengthen critical thinking and systems thinking through accessible tools and learning pathways that help people connect ideas, understand the world more deeply, and transform how they learn.

What We Do

We curate, connect and structure ideas to reveal patterns, reduce complexity, and invite deeper exploration.

Goals

  • Make learning more stimulating and accessible.
  • Improve discoverability and public engagement with peer‑reviewed research.
  • Support teaching with critical, symbolic, narrative, and virtual inquiry tools.
  • Provide mobile‑friendly, shareable topic stories for broader accessibility.
  • Foster cross‑disciplinary exploration via concept‑linked article pathways.
  • Maintain a public‑service, open‑access ethos with transparent processes.
Foci

Explanipedia is a public service initiative against mis/dis/mal‑information combining four domains to help the public make sense of complex ideas:

  • Dialectics - critical enquiry for everyone.
  • Semiotics - meaning in signs and signals via symbol creation.
  • Simulacrics - embodied learning in virtual worlds.
  • Narratics - AI‑assisted narratives for public understanding.

Said shorter: Critique, Symbols, VR, AI Stories.

Said shortest: Spec → Sym → Sim → Sense.

All welcome. All remixable. In service of public understanding.

What kinds of collaborations does Explanipedia focus on?

We work with departments, centres, labs, and research clusters to activate engagement around curated batches of articles. Our model is designed for scalable, meaningful impact rather than one-off requests.

How does Explanipedia support research engagement?

Every article already has a dedicated Explanipedia page with topic enrichment, conceptual mapping, and integration into our four learning modules. Funded activations amplify visibility, deepen public understanding, and generate impact-ready analytics.

Can you activate engagement for individual articles?

We activate articles as part of department- or project-level batches. This ensures coherence, sustainability, and strong impact metrics across a thematic or disciplinary collection.

What does funding actually unlock?

Funding enables prioritized article promotion, structured engagement across the four modules, analytics dashboards, co-created scholarly outputs, and optional interpretive artefacts. It activates an entire engagement program using systems already in place.

What is a SIP?

A SIP (Scholarly Interpretive Piece) is a multimodal interpretive artefact—such as a poem, conceptual audio piece, symbolic montage, or short narrative loop—that translates research themes for wider audiences. SIPs are optional components of funded collaborations.

Do you offer free preview SIPs or sample outputs?

We don’t produce bespoke samples. SIPs (Scholarly Interpretive Pieces) and all advanced engagement features are created once a funded activation is underway.

Can we choose which articles are activated?

Yes. Departments provide a curated list of articles via OpenAlex IDs, and we activate engagement, analytics, and optional SIPs for that selection.

Do you build custom VR environments for institutions?

Our core VR/Symbolic Learning spaces are shared environments, but we’re open to discussing deeper or custom VR collaborations. Feel free to contact us if this is of interest.

What do we need to provide as collaborators?

Typically just a list of OpenAlex article IDs and occasional academic guidance if needed. Explanipedia handles interpretation, engagement design, analytics, and all infrastructure.

Can individual academics collaborate alone?

We usually collaborate at the department, lab, or research-group level. If you are an individual academic, you're welcome to reach out—we can explore possible pathways through your institution.

Can we link or embed Explanipedia pages on our university site?

Yes, and we encourage it. Embedding or linking strengthens engagement metrics and supports institutional impact reporting.

Is this useful for REF/RAE/tenure or impact cases?

Yes. Our analytics, engagement data, interpretive outputs, and module-generated insights are designed to support institutional impact requirements across multiple frameworks.

How long does an engagement activation run?

Most activations run for 12–24 months, but we’re flexible and happy to align with your grant or departmental timeline.
